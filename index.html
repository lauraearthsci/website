Kitesurfing in Tarifa

Tarifa is the kitesurfing capital of Europe. Consistent wind, 38km of white sandy beaches, and world-class schools. Whether you're a pro or a total beginner — this is the place.

🌱

Complete Beginner

From €60–90 / session

Never touched a kite? Perfect. Tarifa's schools are the best in Europe. You'll start on the beach learning kite control, then hit the water. Typical beginner course is 3 sessions of 2 hours (6hrs total). Equipment included.

Group lesson (3-4 people): ~€70 for 3 hours

Private lesson: ~€60–90 per hour

🏄

Experienced Kiter

Gear rental from €45–60

Bring your own gear or rent locally. Full kite + board + wetsuit rental runs about €45–60 for a half day. Tarifa has kites from 3m² to 18m² for every condition. The Poniente and Levante winds are legendary!

Full day rental: ~€70–90

Multi-day discounts available at most shops

What if I don't want to kite?

No worries at all! Tarifa is incredible even without kitesurfing. You can do beach yoga, SUP (stand-up paddleboarding), surf, hike to incredible viewpoints, explore the old town's tapas bars, take a day trip to Morocco (it's visible from the beach!), or simply relax on stunning beaches with a book. This is a wellness weekend — do what feels good. But also, kite lessons a generally 2H long se we won't be gone for too long either ;).

Your Questions, Answered

Everything you need to know. If something's not here, just ask!

Do I need to know how to kitesurf? ▼
Absolutely not! Tarifa is the best place in Europe to learn. Beginner lessons start from scratch — kite control on the beach, then water starts. You'll be amazed how quickly you pick it up. And if kiting isn't your thing, there's beach yoga, surfing, SUP, hiking, and the most beautiful Old Town to explore.
Is Tarifa actually a fun destination? ▼
YES. Tarifa is one of Spain's best-kept secrets. It's the southernmost point of Europe — you can literally see Africa from the beach. The Old Town is charming with winding streets, incredible food, and a relaxed bohemian vibe. The beaches stretch for 38km of white sand. The sunsets are unreal. It's where sporty, wellness-minded Europeans come to play. You'll understand when you get there.
What should I pack? ▼
Light layers for daytime (it can be windy!), swimwear, sunscreen (strong sun), a windbreaker or light jacket, comfortable shoes for cobblestone streets, and something nice for the birthday dinner. If you're an experienced kiter bringing your own gear, the airlines are generally OK with kite bags — check your airline's sports equipment policy. Beginners: everything is provided by the schools.
How does the room situation work? ▼
The main Airbnb has mostly double beds plus two twin beds. Couples will get double rooms, and solo friends can pair up for twins or share a double (if they're comfortable!). We'll sort this once the final group is confirmed. The vibe is relaxed — nobody will be sleeping on a sofa, promise.
What about the last night in a hotel? ▼
The main Airbnb is available until May 3rd, so we move to a boutique hotel for the final night (Sunday). Tarifa Old Town has beautiful small hotels with rooftop terraces and views of Morocco. It'll actually be a nice change of pace — and it means we can check out of the Airbnb stress-free and enjoy the last day without rushing. Rooms around €60–120/night (shareable for couples/friends).
Can I bring a friend? ▼
Yes! The more the merrier. We have a second Airbnb on hold as backup + they can book their own accomodation too. Just let us know ASAP so we can plan accommodation and kite lessons. The cancellation deadline for the second Airbnb is mid April to end of April, so ideally confirm by then.
What if there's no wind for kitesurfing? ▼
Tarifa is one of the windiest places in Europe — it's extremely rare to have zero wind in spring. But even if the wind gods take a day off, the plan still works perfectly: beach day, Old Town exploration, day trip to Tangier (Morocco is 30 minutes by ferry!), hiking, surfing, or just chilling with good food and good people.
When do I need to confirm by? ▼
Ideally by early April so we can finalise rooms, book kite lessons as a group, and decide on the second Airbnb. But if you're a last-minute person — just reach out, we'll make it work!
